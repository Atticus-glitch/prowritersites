3 days later
Email 2 of 3 — Solution
From: Alex Rivera · ProWriterSites <alex@prowritersites.com>
To: {{first_name}} <{{email}}>
Subject: What a strong portfolio site actually does
Timing: Day 4 — Send 3 days after Email 1

Hi {{first_name}},

A portfolio site isn't a digital business card. It's a sales page that works for you 24 hours a day, seven days a week — while you're writing, sleeping, or ignoring your inbox.

Here's what the best freelance portfolio sites get right:

  • They lead with the best work — not all the work. Three to five pieces that represent your highest skill level and most relevant results. Editors don't have time for everything. Give them the highlights and let the writing close the deal.
  • They make the next step impossible to miss. A clear "Hire Me" or "Get in Touch" button above the fold. No hunting. No guessing. The editor knows exactly what to do — and they can do it in under ten seconds.
  • They tell a story, not a timeline. Your bio isn't a list of previous employers. It's a narrative about who you help, how you help them, and why you're the right person for the job. That specificity builds trust before they've read a word of your clips.
  • They load fast and look right on every device. If your site takes more than three seconds to load on mobile, you've lost the editor before they've seen your name. Speed and mobile optimization aren't optional anymore — they're baseline.

That's the benchmark. Does your current online presence clear all four?

In my next email, I'll show you how we can build this for you — quickly, without the technical headache, and for less than you'd expect.
